REGULATION (EU) No 1169/2011 — Food Information to Consumers

KEY REQUIREMENT: Allergen Labeling (Article 21, Annex II)
The 14 major allergens that MUST be declared on ALL food labels in the EU:
1. Cereals containing gluten (wheat, rye, barley, oats, spelt, kamut)
2. Crustaceans (prawns, crabs, lobsters)
3. Eggs
4. Fish
5. Peanuts
6. Soybeans
7. Milk (including lactose)
8. Nuts (almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pecans, brazil, pistachio, macadamia)
9. Celery
10. Mustard
11. Sesame seeds
12. Sulphur dioxide and sulphites (>10mg/kg)
13. Lupin
14. Molluscs (mussels, snails, squid)

Allergens must be emphasized in the ingredients list through font, style, or background color.

KEY REQUIREMENT: Nutritional Declaration (Articles 29-35)
Mandatory per 100g/ml:
- Energy value (kJ/kcal)
- Fat (g)
- Saturates (g)
- Carbohydrate (g)
- Sugars (g)
- Protein (g)
- Salt (g)

May be repeated per portion. Must be presented in tabular format.

KEY REQUIREMENT: Country of Origin (Article 26)
Mandatory when failure to indicate could mislead the consumer. Mandatory for fresh, chilled, and frozen meat of pig, sheep, goat, poultry.

KEY REQUIREMENT: Date Marking (Article 24)
- 'Best before' for non-perishable foods
- 'Use by' for microbiologically perishable foods

KEY REQUIREMENT: Ingredient List (Articles 18-20)
Ingredients in descending order of weight. Quantitative Ingredient Declaration (QUID) required when ingredient is emphasized.

KEY REQUIREMENT: Responsible Operator (Article 8)
The food business operator under whose name the food is marketed, or the importer into the EU, must be identified with address.

KEY REQUIREMENT: Net Quantity (Article 23)
Net quantity in metric units (g, kg, ml, L).

KEY REQUIREMENT: Lot Identification (Article 26)
Each batch must have lot number for traceability.

KEY REQUIREMENT: Minimum Legibility (Article 12)
Mandatory information must be printed in characters with a minimum x-height of 1.2mm.

NOVEL FOOD REGULATION (EU) 2015/2283
Any food not consumed significantly in the EU before 15 May 1997 requires novel food authorization. African ingredients like baobab, moringa, and certain insects require novel food approval before sale in the EU.

EU ORGANIC REGULATION (EU) 2018/848
Products labeled as organic must be certified by an EU-recognized control body. African organic producers need certification equivalence.

EU PESTICIDE REGULATION (EC) 396/2005
Maximum Residue Levels (MRLs) for pesticides in food. African produce must comply with EU MRLs, which are often stricter than local standards.
