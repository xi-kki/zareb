NAFDAC (National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control) — Nigeria Food Labeling Requirements

NAFDAC REGISTRATION
All packaged food products sold in Nigeria must be registered with NAFDAC. Registration number must appear on the product label. Registration requires: product application, label approval, factory inspection, laboratory analysis.

LABELING REQUIREMENTS (Pre-Packaged Food)
Mandatory label information:
1. Product name/description
2. NAFDAC registration number
3. Ingredients list in descending order of proportion
4. Nutritional information (energy, protein, carb, fat)
5. Net weight/volume in metric units
6. Batch/lot number
7. Manufacturing date
8. Expiry date or best-before date
9. Storage conditions
10. Manufacturer name and physical address
11. Country of origin

SPECIFIC NIGERIA RULES
- Labels must be in English language
- Date format: DD/MM/YYYY
- Fortification: wheat flour, vegetable oil, sugar, salt must be fortified per Nigerian standards
- GM foods require mandatory labeling
- Maximum permitted levels for food additives per NAFDAC schedule

NAFDAC TO EU/UK — COMMON GAPS
1. Allergen labeling: NAFDAC does not require EU 14 allergens declaration. EU law does.
2. Nutritional table: NAFDAC requires basic info. EU requires per 100g format with 7 mandatory fields.
3. Responsible person: EU requires an EU-based responsible operator. NAFDAC does not.
4. Best before vs Use by: EU has strict distinction. Nigerian market often uses only expiry date.
5. E-numbers: EU only permits approved additives with E-numbers. Nigerian approval ≠ EU approval.
6. Traceability: EU requires full lot traceability system (one step forward, one step back).
7. Pesticide MRLs: EU MRLs are often stricter than Nigerian limits.

KEBS (Kenya Bureau of Standards) — KEY DIFFERENCES
- KEBS standardization mark (SM) displayed on label
- Product must comply with applicable Kenya Standard (KS)
- Net quantity in metric units
- Country of origin must be declared
